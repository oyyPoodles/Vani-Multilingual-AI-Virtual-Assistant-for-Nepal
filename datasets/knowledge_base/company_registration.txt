Company Registration in Nepal — Guide for VANI Knowledge Base

1. Types of Business Entities
- Sole Proprietorship (Ekal Byapari)
- Partnership Firm
- Private Limited Company (Pvt. Ltd.)
- Public Limited Company (Ltd.)
- Branch Office of Foreign Company
- Non-Governmental Organization (NGO)

2. Private Limited Company Registration
Step 1: Name Reservation
- Apply to Office of Company Registrar (OCR).
- Proposed name must be unique and not misleading.
- Fee: NPR 500.
- Valid for 60 days.

Step 2: Prepare Documents
- Memorandum of Association (MOA)
- Articles of Association (AOA)
- Citizenship copies of all directors/shareholders
- Passport copies (for foreign investors)

Step 3: Register at OCR
- Submit application with MOA, AOA, and fees.
- Minimum capital: NPR 1 (no minimum for Pvt. Ltd.)
- Registration fee: Based on authorized capital.

Step 4: Obtain PAN
- Register at Inland Revenue Department.
- Required for VAT and tax purposes.

Step 5: Local Registration
- Register at local municipality/ward office.
- Obtain business operation license.

3. Registration Fees
- Up to NPR 1 lakh capital: NPR 1,000
- NPR 1-5 lakh: NPR 4,500
- NPR 5 lakh - 25 lakh: NPR 9,500
- NPR 25 lakh - 1 crore: NPR 16,000
- Above NPR 1 crore: NPR 21,000

4. Annual Compliance
- Annual General Meeting (AGM) within 6 months of fiscal year end.
- File annual returns with OCR.
- Tax audit if turnover > NPR 5 million.
- Renew business license annually.

5. Foreign Investment
- Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Act (FITTA).
- Minimum investment: NPR 5 crore (USD 50,000 approx.).
- Approval from Department of Industry.
- Repatriation of profits permitted.

6. Key Contacts

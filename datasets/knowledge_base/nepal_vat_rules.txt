Nepal VAT (Value Added Tax) Regulations — Summary for VANI Knowledge Base

1. VAT Rate
Nepal's standard VAT rate is 13%, applicable to most goods and services.

2. VAT Registration
- Businesses with annual turnover exceeding NPR 5 million must register for VAT.
- Voluntary registration is available for businesses below threshold.
- Registration is done at the Inland Revenue Department (IRD).

3. VAT Filing Deadlines
- Monthly VAT returns must be filed by the 25th of the following month.
- Example: January VAT return is due by February 25th.
- Late filing incurs a penalty of 0.05% per day of the tax due.

4. VAT Exempt Goods and Services
- Basic agricultural products (rice, wheat, lentils, vegetables)
- Educational services
- Health services
- Residential rent
- Public transportation
- Books, newspapers
- Electricity (for domestic use up to certain limit)

5. Zero-Rated Exports
- Goods exported outside Nepal are zero-rated.
- Services provided to entities outside Nepal may qualify.

6. VAT Invoice Requirements
- Seller's PAN (Permanent Account Number)
- Buyer's PAN (for B2B transactions)
- Date of transaction
- Description of goods/services
- Quantity and unit price
- Total amount and VAT amount separately stated

7. Input Tax Credit
- VAT paid on business purchases can be claimed as input tax credit.
- Credit is available only for VAT-registered businesses.
- Personal expenses and entertainment costs are not eligible.

8. Penalties
- Late filing: 0.05% per day
- Understatement of tax: 50% of underpaid amount
- Non-registration: NPR 10,000 or actual tax, whichever is higher

9. Important Contacts
- Inland Revenue Department: www.ird.gov.np
- VAT Helpline: 1660-01-60060
- Regional IRD offices available in all 7 provinces

10. Recent Changes (2024-2025)
- Digital services by foreign providers now subject to VAT
- E-invoicing being gradually implemented
- Threshold for mandatory registration remains NPR 5 million

Nepal Tourism Permits — Guide for VANI Knowledge Base

1. TIMS Card (Trekkers' Information Management System)
- Required for most trekking routes in Nepal.
- Fee: NPR 2,000 for individual trekkers, NPR 1,000 through agency.
- Obtain from: Nepal Tourism Board office (Kathmandu/Pokhara) or TAAN office.
- Photo and passport copy required.

2. National Park Entry Permits
- Sagarmatha (Everest) National Park: NPR 3,000 (SAARC), USD 30 (others)
- Annapurna Conservation Area: NPR 3,000 (SAARC), USD 30 (others)
- Langtang National Park: NPR 3,000 (SAARC), USD 30 (others)
- Chitwan National Park: NPR 2,000 (SAARC), USD 20 (others)
- Nepali citizens: NPR 100-250 depending on park

3. Restricted Area Permits
- Upper Mustang: USD 500 per 10 days (min 2 persons with guide)
- Upper Dolpo: USD 500 per 10 days
- Manaslu: USD 100 per week (Sep-Nov), USD 75 (Dec-Aug)
- Kanchenjunga: USD 20 per week
- Must be arranged through registered trekking agency.

4. Visa Information
- On-arrival visa available at Tribhuvan International Airport.
- 15 days: USD 30
- 30 days: USD 50
- 90 days: USD 125
- SAARC nationals: Free visa.
- Chinese nationals: Free visa.
- Extension: Up to 150 days per calendar year.

5. Popular Trekking Routes
- Everest Base Camp Trek: 12-14 days, moderate-difficult
- Annapurna Circuit: 15-20 days, moderate
- Annapurna Base Camp: 7-10 days, moderate
- Langtang Valley: 7-10 days, moderate
- Manaslu Circuit: 14-18 days, difficult
- Poon Hill: 4-5 days, easy

7. Transportation
- Domestic flights: Buddha Air, Yeti Airlines, Saurya Airlines
- Tourist buses: Greenline, Himalayan Java, Mountain Overland
- Major airports: Tribhuvan (KTM), Pokhara, Bharatpur, Biratnagar
